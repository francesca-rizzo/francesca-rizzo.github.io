About Me

I am a DAWN/Interactions Fellow at the Cosmic Dawn Center , a research center located in Copenhagen at the Niels Bohr Institute.

I did my PhD at the Max Planck Institute for Astrophysics in Garching (Germany) under the supervision of Dr Simona Vegetti and Prof Simon White. You can find here my PhD thesis, "A strong gravitational lensing view on the dynamical properties of high-redshift star-forming galaxies".

For my Master Degree in Astrophysics and Cosmology, I studied at University of Bologna (Italy) and I obtained my Bachelor in Physics at University of Pisa (Italy).

I grew up in Casarano, a village in Puglia, a fabulous region in southern Italy.

Research

I am interested in the ways in which galaxies form and evolve across cosmic time. In particular, my research focuses on galactic dynamics, that is the study of the motion of gas and/or stars within galaxies. Through this kind of study, I learn how different physical processes shaped the variety of galaxies that we observe today.

My research is focused on high-redshift galaxies, young and distant objects that give us information about the early stage of the Universe. Detailed studies of these objects are almost impossible for current telescopes, because they appear very small and faint. I overcome this limitation by using nearby galaxies as magnification glasses that give distorted and magnified images of the background objects. In this case, the glass is the mass of the foreground galaxy that causes a distortion of the space-time, so that the light of the background galaxy is distorted. This phenomenon is called gravitational lensing and was predicted by Einstein and observed for the first time by Eddington in 1919.
